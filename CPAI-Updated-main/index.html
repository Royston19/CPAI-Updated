What do we do?

Empowering every step, CPAI stands with you.

CPAI is an NGO devoted to the wellbeing and upliftment of People with Different Abilities (PDAs). It is a thought child of Padma Bhushan awardee, late Dr. N.H Wadia and late Dr. V. Kini whose daughter was also a PDA. The Cerebral Palsy Association of India was started in 1968 to care for many other people like her.

Today our centres house 80 people; and addresses not only their educational needs but also makes efforts towards their holistic development. CPAI works earnestly towards the rehabilitation and welfare of PDAs for their advancement without any discrimination of sex, class, and creed.

Project Shikshan

Education is one of the strongest pillars on which a person can sustain, a community can be built, and a nation can lead. We want to lead our underprivileged children towards a better life, and nothing else than education can be the right way.
We have identified a need to support. Many children have lost their earning parent, or the earning parent has lost their job during COVID-19. In such a circumstance, children have to disengage with their studies, finding it difficult to secure admission or pay fees. The worst affected are the single mother families.
We aim to support 50 such underprivileged beneficiaries with our Shikshan initiative, by funding a child’s entire schooling expense. An educated child could lead a fulfilled life and can also take care of their family.
We intend to extend our support through- Financial educational support to children deprived of school or dropouts. Skills share for independent development and community growth. Wish to sponsor a child’s education?

Frequently Asked Questions

What is cerebral palsy?

Cerebral palsy is a group of permanent movement disorders that appear in early childhood. It affects muscle coordination and body movement, often caused by damage to the developing brain before or during birth.

What services does CPAI provide?

CPAI offers a range of services including therapy programs, assistive technology support, advocacy, educational resources, and community events aimed at improving the quality of life for individuals with cerebral palsy and other disabilities.

How can I get involved with CPAI?

There are several ways to get involved with CPAI, including volunteering for events, donating to support our programs, participating in fundraising activities, and spreading awareness about cerebral palsy and disability rights.

What are the symptoms of cerebral palsy?

Symptoms of cerebral palsy vary depending on the type and severity of the condition but may include impaired movement, muscle stiffness, poor coordination, tremors, and speech difficulties.
